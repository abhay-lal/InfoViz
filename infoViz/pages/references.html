Data Statement

Data Statement:

The data used in this analysis was acquired from various open source links on the internet, with a significant portion retrieved from Yahoo Finance. The data includes multiple features that capture different aspects of the stock market and economic indicators. The primary feature extracted from the data is the time stamp, which represents the specific time at which each price point or economic data point was recorded. This enables the analysis of trends and patterns over different timeframes. In addition to the time stamp, several economic indicators were included in the dataset.

These indicators include:
  • Unemployment data: The dataset incorporates information from the Employment Situation report, which is typically released on the third Friday after the conclusion of the reference week. This report provides insights into the unemployment rate, offering a measure of economic health and labor market conditions.
  • Consumer Price Index (CPI): The dataset includes CPI data, which represents the cost of a fixed basket of goods and services. The CPI is calculated based on 299 items and is released monthly. It provides an indication of inflationary trends and changes in purchasing power.
  • P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings Ratio): The dataset incorporates the P/E ratio, which is commonly used to value companies and assess their relative overvaluation or undervaluation. The P/E ratio compares a company's stock price to its earnings per share (EPS) and helps investors gauge the market's expectations for future earnings growth.
  • Opening, High, Low, and Closing Prices: The dataset includes the opening, high, low, and closing prices for the S&P 500 index or other relevant indices. These price points provide information about the trading range and the overall performance of the market within a given time frame.
  • Industrial Production Index (IPI): The dataset includes the IPI, which measures the levels of production and capacity in the manufacturing, mining, electric, and gas industries relative to a base year. The IPI offers insights into industrial activity and is typically published by the Federal Reserve Board on a monthly basis.
  • Treasury Yield: The dataset incorporates the market yield on U.S. Treasury Securities at a 10-year constant maturity. This yield is quoted on an investment basis and is updated on the 11th business day of each month. It provides an indication of the prevailing interest rates and market sentiment towards government debt.

    • References:

    The data utilized in this analysis was primarily sourced from Yahoo Finance, which provides a wide range of financial and economic data. Yahoo Finance offers access to historical stock prices, economic indicators, and other financial information that are relevant to understanding market behavior. Additionally, open-source links from the internet were explored to gather supplementary data. These sources could include reputable financial websites, government publications, economic data providers, and other reliable sources of market and economic information. It's important to note that the specific references for the individual data points used in this analysis may vary based on the particular sources and datasets accessed. Care was taken to utilize reputable and reliable sources to ensure the accuracy and integrity of the data.
